<p class="dropcap"><span class="dropcap-letter" aria-hidden="true">A</span> lot of the data that could warn us about disasters already exists. Population movement shows up in call detail records, environmental stress in satellite imagery, vulnerability in ground surveys. Cornell's Thought Summit brought that research into one room, along with the funders and field partners who could act on it; the summit's output is a white paper on where predictive disaster-resilience systems stand and what they need next.</p>
