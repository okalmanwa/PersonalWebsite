## The question

Much of the data that could provide early warning for disasters already exists. Population movement shows up in call detail records, environmental stress in satellite imagery, and vulnerability in ground surveys. But it lives in different formats, resolutions, and institutions. Can we fuse it into systems that give humanitarian responders lead time?

## The white paper

At CIDER (Cornell Institute for Digital Engagement and Resilience), I'm co-authoring a white paper for Cornell's "Data Science to Build Resilience and Improve Humanitarian Response" Thought Summit. The paper surveys how researchers are answering that question.

## My role

I don't build these systems; I synthesize the research submitted to the summit into a coherent argument:

- Reading and synthesizing the submitted papers, spanning CDR feature construction, satellite imagery pipelines, and multi-modal data fusion.
- Researching predictive systems for disaster resilience, with a focus on data engineering and early-warning design.
- Translating technical ML findings into policy-relevant narratives for academic and practitioner audiences.

## Beyond the writing

Half the work is making the synthesis land. I engage the summit's funders and partners directly, because the research only matters here if institutions act on it.
