INTRODUCTION

Jambo! (Hello) I'm Carey, but my friends call me Okal. I'm a first-gen undergrad from Kisii, Kenya, studying Information Science at Cornell University.

I have a diverse range of interests that encompass information architecture, front-end web development, and web application development on both the client and server sides, along with a strong focus on search engine optimization. My technical skills include Python, shell scripting, and proficiency in front-end technologies such as HTML5, CSS3, Vanilla JavaScript, ReactJS and JQuery. Additionally, I have hands-on experience with various Python libraries like NumPy, Beautiful Soup, Pandas, Requests, Seaborn, Matplotlib for data analysis and visualization, as well as expertise in SQL for efficient database management.

Apart from my studies, I'm an active member of the Under Represented Minorities in Computing group on campus. I'm also involved in the Adventist Christian Fellowship. And of course, I really enjoy cracking jokes and meeting new people.

INITIATIVES

As I was about to finish high school and get ready for what's next, I noticed a big problem at Darajambili Primary school, my alma mater. There weren't enough math tutors because they couldn't afford them. This left a gap in education that needed to be filled. Feeling a strong desire to help, I decided to do something about it. Instead of just waiting, I used my weekends to volunteer and help the students there with math.

I didn't rush it, though. I took my time and used my passion for teaching to make math easier to understand for the students. After each session, I could see a change. The students started to understand math better, and their grades got better too. This wasn't just about teaching math, it was also about making the students feel more confident and excited about learning.

I developed a Visual Basic for Applications hotel management system for Ufanisi Resort in Kisii. This system facilitates guest bookings, generates reports, and effectively manages inventories for the hotel.

I also incorporated advanced data analysis features into the hotel management system, enabling Ufanisi Resort to make informed decisions. Additionally, the user-friendly interface I designed ensures seamless navigation and efficient utilization of the system's functionalities. An unlicensed version of the system can be accessed here .

In response to the challenges of insecurity and unemployment in our community, I founded an organization called "Vijana na Computer" with a clear mission: to empower local youth by teaching them programming. As the founder and main instructor, my role was to guide a group of 10 enthusiastic students through the world of web development. This journey involved learning important skills like CSS, HTML5, and JavaScript, which is a dynamic programming language.

But my responsibilities went beyond just teaching technical concepts. I also shared practical knowledge about things like Twitter Bootstrap, hosting projects on GitHub, and the strategies behind improving a website's visibility on search engines (known as SEO). It wasn't only about teaching technical skills; I aimed to provide a well-rounded education. Guiding these talented students has been an incredible privilege.
